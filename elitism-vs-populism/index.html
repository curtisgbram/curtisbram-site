Book

Elitism vs. Populism

A short book on democratic commitment, majoritarian abuse, and whether confidence in elites is actually warranted.

Cambridge page KERA interview Podcast interview
Summary

Who is more committed to democracy: the people or the elites?

Critics of populism and advocates of elitist democracy often place more trust in political elites than in the general public. The book argues that this confidence may be misplaced. Across five experiments with local politicians, state legislators, and members of the public, the evidence points to a similar willingness across groups to entrench their own side’s power when given the opportunity.

This tendency is strongest among committed ideologues, politicians in highly competitive districts, and those who perceive opponents as especially threatening. The book challenges a simple mass-versus-elite story by showing surprisingly small differences in undemocratic attitudes.

The practical implication is that safeguarding democracy likely depends less on hoping one group will be normatively purer than another and more on strengthening institutional restraints that limit majority abuses.
